Early Programming

From an early age I had always loved playing games and wanted to learn to make them.

At around 10 or 11 I got into writing some very simple Batch files to run simple commmands
I got into Python programming around 13 which I used throughout my Computer Science GCSE
and A-Levels.

I had wanted to get into some proper games development, I first tried with Unreal engine 4
but without knowing where to start or following any proper tutorials I very little success.
When I went to university open days, I was given a live tutorial on how to make a very simple game.
As soon as I got home I tried to recreate the tutorial and ended up creating some simple projects.

Within a few months I had created a couple of simple Android mobile games that I released on the
Android Play Store!


University

I studied games programming at Falmouth Games Academy.
I learned the interesting and effective ways to create functionality for games,
including character controllers, game artificial intelligence, menus and lots of other useful things.
I took AI programming as a specialism subject during university because that was the area
that I thought I was weakest in. The course helped me to improve and refine my skills.

I shall be graduating University later this year and starting an indie games company with other
students that I have been working with.
